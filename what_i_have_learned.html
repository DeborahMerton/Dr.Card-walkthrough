What I have learned

I wish I could say that I learned all I needed to for this class, and that I feel good about it. Unfortunately, that's not the case. I will be completely honest with myself and say that this course has been way outside of my weight class. I can read professional journal articles and pick out the most important points the author is trying to make, but designing a website? Not so much.

I'm not sure what has gotten in my way with this course. I have a feeling that it has been a lack of a basic computer-related vocabulary more than anything else. I need to pay attention to old words that are used in a context I haven't seen before. For example, in my initial DITA project, I did not realize that the word "typing" in "Darwin Information Typing Architecture" did NOT mean "typing" as in the verb "I'm typing up an email" but rather "typing" as in "I need to put this data into different types."

I have come to the realization that I have a gap in my basic understanding of computers, software and applications, and any type of web design. That has hampered my learning at times, as I don't always know the correct questions to ask in order to move forward in my projects. For example, I had to ask Dr. Card today (Friday) what a "file path" was and why it was important for my DITA project. I am used to programs like Micosoft Word, where I save my documents right inside the program. That is not how oXygen-Editor, my DITA program of choice, works. I needed to designate a folder on my desktop in which to store my project. I didn't remember that until late Wednesday night, with my project due on Friday!

Out of all the assignments and projects we covered in this class, I enjoyed the client project the most. The group I was in was very supportive and knowledgable about the topic of web design. Brian Le Lay was especially helpful, as he's been working with computers and designing websites since he was eleven. It was a relief to me to have other people I could rely on, not just myself. I was also happy that I could add my own perspective to the project, in this case, what type of website would work well for users age 50 and above.
